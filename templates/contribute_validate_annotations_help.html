Task instructions

Thank you for participating in the Tagathon’s first edition! Please read these guidelines carefully before annotating. If you have any questions, do not hesitate to ask an organizer.

A bit of context: As you may know, we are trying to build a dataset with sounds from Freesound following the AudioSet Ontology. As starting point, we have populated the AudioSet sound categories with Freesound clips based on tag matching (manually assigning Freesound tags to each category).

Why are we doing this Tagathon?

The main goal is to validate annotations obtained from the tag matching process, which will allow us to:

  • Estimate the quality of the sound categories coming from that process
  • Take the first step towards building a Freesound Dataset
  • Gather feedback on the ontology, the audio content and the annotation procedure
  • Test the annotation tool and think of improvements

Tagathon guidelines

To organize the Tagathon, we have split the sound categories of the AudioSet Ontology into several groups that can be seen in this spreadsheet (surrounded by red boxes). Categories in each group should be validated by one person. These groups vary in size, so that you can choose one based on how much time you think that you have available to help. For each category you will be presented with pages of 12 sounds. For each sound clip, you will be asked if this sound contains the item described by the category. You will find more detailed description below, in the section How to validate a category.

You should validate until the number of validated annotations reaches 72. You can see this number in the table at the top of a validation page, after choosing a category. Some categories may contain fewer sounds (< 72). In this case, just validate all annotations (the interface will tell you whenever it is the case).

How to proceed

1

Log in to https://datasets.freesound.org/fsd/

2

Go to the spreadsheet and choose a group to validate (please write your name next to it). Understand the type of categories that you are about to validate.

3

Use the hyperlinks in the spreadsheet to access the validation page for each category or browse the categories in the main Validate category annotations page.

4

Start listening and validating the annotations!

How to validate a category

1

Acclimatise yourself with your category and its related categories. The section at the top of the page gives you an overview of the category and its meaning.

Hierarchy

A path that tells you where you are in the ontology, for example Animal > Domestic animals, pets > Cat. Note that in some cases there may be several paths, ie, several parents for your category. For example, Hiss:
     Animal > Domestic animals, pets > Cat > Hiss
     Animal > Wild animals > Snake > Hiss
Please consider all the parents of your category when annotating.

Direct children

Children of the category you are seeing. Useful for understanding the context of the category that you are in, and navigating through children. This section will not appear if you are on a final leaf category.

Description

An explanation of the meaning and limits of the category. Please read and understand the description of the category before annotating.

Note that some categories are highlighted in red. You should not validate them!
Feel free to also check AudioSet website to explore the Ontology

2

Put your headphones, adjust sound level comfortably and rate sounds in the category according to the formulated question: Is <sound category> present in the following sounds? For some categories, a few examples are provided (please note they don’t necessarily cover all the possible cases). There are 4 possible answers:

Present and predominant

The type of sound described is clearly present in the audio clip and it is predominant, either isolated from other types of sound or with low/mild background noise.

Present but not predominant

The type of sound described is present, but the audio clip also contains other salient types of sound and/or strong background noise.

Not Present

The type of sound described is not present in the audio clip.

Unsure

I am not sure whether the type of sound described is present or not.

3

Are you finding it difficult? Feel free to access Freesound through the link at the top right corner of every sound () to check the sound’s metadata (description and tags).

4

Do you have any feedback about the category? You can add comments in the text box at the bottom of the page.

5

Submit your ratings for this page and either:

  • continue with the next page in this category (until 72 validated) or...
  • move to your next category :)

6

Please take a 10-minute break each hour, and take a longer break to rest if you reach 3 hours.

You can click the help button next to the formulated question to see a short version of these guidelines. If you have any general comments, eg, annotation tool improvements, annotation process, ontology aspects, etc., do not hesitate to share your opinion with us. Your feedback is very valuable!

Thanks a lot for helping out and happy annotating! Take a rest every now and then and don’t forget there are food and drinks!

FAQ

How should I rate human imitations?

If they sound like the original source, they should be rated as one type of Present.
Bad imitations should be rated as Not Present.

How should I rate synthesized sounds?

If they sound like the original source, they should be rated as one type of Present.
Bad synthetic sounds should be rated as Not Present.

How should I rate heavily processed sounds (e.g., using effects)?

Easily recognizable sounds should be rated as as one type of Present.
Sounds that are very different from the original source should be rated as Not Present.

How should I rate badly recorded sounds (e.g., clipping and/or strong noise)?

Easily recognizable sounds should be rated as as one type of Present.
Sounds that are very different from the original source should be rated as Not Present.

How should I rate reversed sounds?

Easily recognizable sounds should be rated as as one type of Present.
Sounds that are very different from the original source should be rated as Not Present.
